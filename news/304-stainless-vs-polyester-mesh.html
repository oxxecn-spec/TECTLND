304 Stainless Steel Mesh vs Polyester: Which Lasts Longer?

May 2, 2026 7 min read 304 SS, Polyester

Choosing the right mesh material is critical for longevity and performance. Two options dominate the US market: 304 stainless steel mesh and PET polyester mesh. This guide compares them side‑by‑side.

304 Stainless Steel Mesh

Heat‑treated, woven stainless steel wire (typically 0.5‑0.6mm thick).

  • Pros: Virtually indestructible, rust‑proof, pet‑proof (claw/bite resistant), long lifespan (20+ years).
  • Cons: Expensive (2‑3x polyester), slightly less airflow, slightly more visible.

PET Polyester Mesh

UV‑stabilised woven polyester.

  • Pros: Affordable, high airflow (85‑90%), nearly invisible, soft to touch.
  • Cons: Can be scratched by cats, can sag over time, lifespan 5‑10 years.

Which One for Your US Home?

  • Coastal states (FL, TX, CA): 304 stainless steel – resists salt air corrosion.
  • Homes with dogs/cats: 304 stainless steel.
  • Budget‑conscious / normal use: PET polyester is perfectly adequate.

Conclusion

Both materials are excellent. Choose stainless steel for ultimate durability and pet protection; choose polyester for budget and maximum airflow. MBORMC offers both, custom‑cut to your exact size.
