Mosquito Screens for Manchester – Terraced Houses, Flats & Modern Apartments

May 2, 2026 5 min read Manchester, City Guide

Manchester’s weather is famously variable – but summer evenings still bring mosquitoes, especially near canals and green spaces. From red‑brick Victorian terraces to glass‑fronted Salford Quays apartments, each home needs a slightly different insect screen solution. This guide covers the best options.

Victorian & Edwardian Terraced Houses

These homes often have tall sash windows or casement windows. For sash windows, retractable screens mounted at the top of the lower sash are popular. They roll down when the window is open and disappear when closed. For outward‑opening casement windows, adhesive velcro screens stick to the interior frame without interfering with handles – perfect for renters and owners alike.

Many terraced houses have small courtyard back doors. A magnetic mosquito curtain on the back door allows you to bring the washing in without letting flies inside.

Modern Apartments (Salford Quays, Deansgate, City Centre)

High‑rise flats have sliding windows and balcony doors. Drilling is usually prohibited, so top‑hung pleated screens (no floor track) are ideal – they slide on an overhead rail and have no contact with the floor. They maintain the clean modern look and avoid violating tenancy agreements. For sliding windows, a retractable screen that mounts on the stationary side is a good option.

Suburban Family Homes (Didsbury, Chorlton, Altrincham)

These homes often have French doors opening onto gardens. Bi‑parting folding screens (concertina) are the best solution. Two panels slide from the centre to the sides, folding into a compact stack. They cover the entire opening without a centre post. For homes with children and pets, choose 304 stainless steel mesh – it withstands scratching and pushing.

Screens for Canal‑Side Properties

Living near the Manchester Ship Canal or Bridgewater Canal means higher mosquito pressure. Fine mesh (22 holes per inch) is essential to block midges. Also consider solar screens – they reflect UV and keep your home cooler on the rare hot days, reducing the need for air conditioning.

DIY Installation Tips

Manchester’s housing stock has non‑standard window sizes. Measure your windows carefully (width and height in mm). Order custom screens from MBORMC – we cut to your exact dimensions. Adhesive screens can be installed in 10 minutes; retractable and folding systems require basic tools but come with video instructions.
