Cardiff Mosquito Screens – Victorian Terraces, Cardiff Bay & Conservation Areas

May 2, 2026 5 min read Cardiff, Welsh Capital

Cardiff, the Welsh capital, blends rich Victorian heritage with modern waterfront living. From student‑friendly flats in Cathays to elegant houses in Pontcanna and the dynamic Cardiff Bay, each area needs tailored insect screen solutions.

Victorian & Edwardian Terraces (Pontcanna, Canton, Roath)

These characterful homes feature original sash or casement windows with detailed timber frames. Adhesive velcro screens are ideal – they require no drilling, are fully removable, and invisible from the street. For a more permanent but heritage‑sensitive option, retractable screens that mount inside the window recess using tension (no screws) are perfect.

Cardiff Bay & Waterfront Apartments (Mermaid Quay, The Flourish, Century Wharf)

Modern flats with large sliding doors and floor‑to‑ceiling windows face the bay’s salty air. 304 stainless steel mesh is essential to resist salt corrosion. Choose top‑hung pleated screens (no floor track) – they slide on an overhead rail, have no bottom tripping hazard, and maintain the clean, contemporary look.

Student & Rental Accommodation (Cathays, Roath, City Centre)

With two universities, Cardiff has many renters. Magnetic mosquito nets for doors and adhesive screens for windows are perfect – no tools, no damage, and you can take them when you move.

Conservation Areas (Castle Quarter, Lisvane, Cyncoed)

External changes are restricted. Internal screens are permitted. Use white or grey frames that blend with window joinery. Adhesive screens require no planning permission at all.

Mosquito Pressure & Mesh Choice

The River Taff and Cardiff Bay’s still water attract mosquitoes. Use fine mesh (20‑22 holes per inch). For bay‑facing properties, 304 stainless steel is highly recommended for longevity. PET polyester is fine for inland homes.
