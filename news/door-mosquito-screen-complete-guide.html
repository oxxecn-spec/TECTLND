Door Mosquito Screen – Keep Bugs Out, Let Fresh Air In

2 May 2026 8 min read Door Screen, Sliding Screen

A door mosquito screen is one of the most important investments for any home. It allows you to keep your door open for fresh air and easy access without inviting a swarm of insects inside. This guide covers all types of door screens – retractable, folding, magnetic, and fixed – and helps you choose the right one for your specific door.

Types of Door Mosquito Screens

1. Retractable Screen Doors

These roll up into a cassette mounted on the side of the door frame. When you need the screen, pull it across; when not, it disappears entirely. Ideal for single doors (e.g., front door or back door). Some models are motorised or self‑closing.

2. Folding (Concertina) Screen Doors

Perfect for wide openings like patio doors or French doors. The screen folds like an accordion and slides to one side (single leaf) or both sides (bi‑parting). They take up less side space than retractable cassettes and can cover very wide spans (up to 4 metres).

3. Magnetic Screen Doors

The simplest and cheapest option. Two mesh panels hang from the top of the door frame and meet in the middle with magnets. You can walk through freely, and the magnets snap shut behind you. Great for kitchen doors, back doors, or rental properties because they require no drilling.

4. Fixed (Permanent) Screen Doors

A rigid frame with mesh is hinged or sliding. The most durable option but cannot be removed easily. Common for front doors where a screen door is left in place year‑round.

Which Door Type Calls for Which Screen?

  • Single front/back door: Retractable or fixed screen door. Retractable is more aesthetic.
  • Sliding glass patio door: Folding screen (concertina) or retractable (if width ≤ 1200mm). For very wide doors, folding is best.
  • French doors (double doors): Bi‑parting folding screen (two panels sliding outward).
  • Kitchen or utility door: Magnetic screen – hands‑free and cheap.
  • Garage door (side entrance): Heavy‑duty folding screen with 304 stainless steel mesh.

Materials for Door Screens

For high‑traffic doors, choose 304 stainless steel mesh – it withstands pets, kids, and daily use. PET mesh is fine for low‑traffic doors but will not survive scratching or pushing. Fibreglass should be avoided entirely for doors.

Installation Considerations

Magnetic screens are DIY (5 minutes). Retractable and folding screens require basic tools (screwdriver, drill, spirit level). Most come with detailed instructions. For rental properties, consider hanging (top‑mounted) screens that use no floor track – the screen hangs from an overhead rail and has no bottom tripping hazard.

Cost Ranges (USD)

  • Magnetic curtain: $15‑40
  • Fixed aluminum screen door: $50‑150
  • Retractable screen door (single): $150‑400
  • Folding screen door (custom, PET mesh): $250‑600
  • Folding screen door (custom, 304 stainless steel): $350‑800+

Conclusion

Choosing the right door mosquito screen depends on your door type, budget, and how often you use the door. For most homes, a folding or retractable screen offers the best balance of convenience and durability.
