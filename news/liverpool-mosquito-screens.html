Liverpool Mosquito Screens – Georgian Houses, Waterfront Apartments & Conservation Areas

May 2, 2026 5 min read Liverpool, Georgian

Liverpool’s stunning Georgian townhouses and modern waterfront apartments demand insect screens that protect without detracting from architecture. High humidity near the Mersey and conservation rules add complexity. This guide covers the best solutions for Liverpool homes.

Georgian & Victorian Townhouses (Canning, Georgian Quarter, Sefton Park)

These homes feature tall sash windows and intricate woodwork. Drilling into heritage frames is often prohibited. Adhesive velcro screens stick to the interior frame and are invisible from outside. For a more permanent but still reversible solution, retractable screens that mount on the window lining (not the historic timber) are ideal.

Waterfront Apartments (Albert Dock, Liverpool ONE, Wirral side)

Modern flats have large sliding doors with views of the Mersey. Top‑hung pleated screens (no floor track) preserve the clean aesthetic – they slide on an overhead rail and have no bottom tripping hazard. For sliding windows, retractable screens that roll up into a slim cassette are unobtrusive.

High Humidity & Salt Air

The river Mersey brings salty, humid air. Fibreglass screens corrode quickly. Choose 304 stainless steel mesh for doors and windows facing the water. For inland properties, PET polyester is sufficient but avoid fibreglass entirely.

Conservation Areas (Hope Street, Rodney Street, etc.)

Liverpool has many conservation areas where external changes are controlled. Internal screens are permitted. Use white or grey frames that match the window joinery. Adhesive screens are completely invisible from outside and require no planning permission.
