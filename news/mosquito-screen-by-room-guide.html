How to Choose Mosquito Screens for Every Room Type

17 May 2026 7 min read Window Screen, Door Screen, Anti Mosquito

Not all mosquito screens are the same – what works perfectly for a bedroom may be wrong for a kitchen or patio door. This guide walks you through each room and recommends the best window mosquito screen or door mosquito screen for that space.

1. Bedrooms – The Highest Priority

Mosquitoes are most active at dawn and dusk – exactly when you sleep. A single mosquito can ruin a night's rest. For bedrooms, you need a fine insect protection screen (20‑22 mesh) that blocks even the tiniest biting insects. Retractable roll‑up screens are ideal because they disappear during the day, preserving your view. Fixed screens also work well if you don't mind the permanent mesh.

Recommendation: Retractable window mosquito screen with PET or 304 mesh. Black or dark grey mesh is less visible at night.

2. Living Rooms & Lounges – Aesthetic Matters

Living rooms often have large windows or sliding doors. You want insect protection without ruining the view or making the room feel caged. Folding screen doors (concertina style) or retractable screens are excellent choices. For a minimalist look, choose a frame colour that matches your window trim and a fine mesh that is barely noticeable.

Recommendation: Bi‑parting folding screen for French doors, or a large retractable screen for sliding glass doors.

3. Kitchens – Grease & Heat Resistance

Kitchens face unique challenges: steam, grease, and frequent opening. A standard insect screen may accumulate grease and become hard to clean. Here, a crystal clear PVC folding panel is a game‑changer – it wipes clean easily, resists grease, and remains transparent. For windows, a simple adhesive velcro screen works well for ventilation while cooking.

Recommendation: PVC folding door screen or self‑adhesive mosquito mesh for kitchen windows.

4. Bathrooms – Privacy & Ventilation

Bathrooms need good airflow to prevent mould, but you also want privacy and insect protection. A fixed insect screen on the window (or a retractable screen that can be rolled up when you need to clean the glass) is sufficient. If your bathroom has a small vent window, a magnetic mosquito curtain can be installed easily.

Recommendation: Standard insect screen on window, or a no‑drill adhesive screen for easy removal.

5. Patio Doors & Balconies – High Traffic, No Floor Tracks

Patio doors are opened dozens of times a day. You need a durable screen that won't be damaged by pets or children. Also, many balconies have tiled floors that cannot accommodate bottom tracks. The solution is a hanged (top‑mounted) pleated screen – it slides on an overhead rail and has no floor track. Alternatively, a folding screen door (concertina) with a bottom guide rail can be used if the floor allows.

Recommendation: Hanged pleated screen (no floor track) or heavy‑duty folding screen with 304 stainless steel mesh.

6. Home Office – Quiet & Clear View

In a home office, you need concentration and natural light. Anything that obstructs the view or creates glare is distracting. A high‑transparency insect screen (20‑22 mesh woven from thin PET threads) is nearly invisible. Roll‑up screens allow you to open the window fully when needed.

Recommendation: Retractable window mosquito screen with ultra‑fine PET mesh.

7. Garage & Workshop – Heavy Duty Protection

Garages often have large openings and are prone to dust and insects. You need a rugged solution. A folding screen door with 304 stainless steel mesh can withstand bumps from vehicles, tools, and even pets. For a garage side door, a magnetic mosquito curtain is a cheap and effective option.

Recommendation: 304 stainless steel folding screen or magnetic curtain for garage entry doors.
