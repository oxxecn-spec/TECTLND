How to Clean & Maintain Your Mosquito Screens

May 2, 2026 5 min read Maintenance, Cleaning

With proper care, your mosquito screens will last for years and perform optimally. This guide covers cleaning routines, seasonal storage, and simple repairs.

Why Regular Cleaning Matters

Dust, pollen, and grime accumulate on mesh, reducing airflow and visibility. In coastal areas, salt residue can accelerate corrosion on metal components if not rinsed.

Cleaning PET or Fibreglass Mesh

  • Vacuum: Use a soft brush attachment to remove loose dust.
  • Wash: Mix mild dish soap with warm water. Dip a soft sponge or cloth and gently wipe both sides.
  • Rinse: Use a garden hose with low pressure – never a pressure washer.
  • Dry: Air dry or pat with a microfiber cloth.

Cleaning 304 Stainless Steel Mesh

Stainless steel is easier to clean. Use a soft brush and soapy water. For stubborn spots, a non‑abrasive cleaner is safe. Rinse thoroughly to avoid soap residue.

When to Remove Screens for Deep Cleaning

Adhesive screens and magnetic curtains can be taken down, washed in a bathtub with mild soap, and air dried. Fixed frames are best cleaned in place.

Seasonal Storage (Retractable & Magnetic Screens)

If you live in a region with cold winters, remove retractable screens (or store the cassettes) and magnetic nets. Clean, dry thoroughly, roll loosely, and store in a dry place. Avoid folding sharply to prevent creases.

Repairing Small Holes

For small tears in PET or fibreglass, use mesh repair tape (available at hardware stores). For stainless steel, you may need to replace the entire panel.

When to Replace Your Screens

Signs include large holes, sagging mesh, rusted frames, or severely reduced airflow. High‑quality screens from MBORMC typically last 5‑20 years depending on material.

Conclusion

Regular maintenance keeps your screens effective and looking good. For custom replacement screens, contact MBORMC.
