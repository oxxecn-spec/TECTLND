Sliding patio doors are common in American homes – from California to New York. But they’re also the biggest entry point for mosquitoes. A good sliding screen door must be durable, easy to operate, and visually unobtrusive. This guide compares retractable, folding, and hanging screens for US patios.

Types of Screens for Sliding Doors

1. Retractable Screen Doors

A spring‑loaded screen rolls into a cassette mounted on the side. Pull it across when needed; it disappears when not. Ideal for doors up to 3 feet wide (single). Not recommended for very wide doors.

2. Folding (Concertina) Screens

The best choice for wide doors (up to 12 feet). The screen folds like an accordion and slides sideways. Available as single leaf or bi‑parting (two panels). Durable and can be made with 304 stainless steel mesh.

3. Hanging Pleated Screens (No Floor Track)

For tiled balconies or floors where you don’t want a bottom track. The screen hangs from an overhead rail and glides smoothly. Completely floor‑free – no trip hazard.

Which Material for Your Climate?

304 stainless steel: Coastal areas (Florida, Texas coast) – rust‑proof, pet‑proof.
PET polyester: Inland, normal use – lighter and cheaper.

Cost Ranges (USD)

  • Retractable (single door): $150‑400
  • Folding screen (custom, PET): $250‑600
  • Folding screen (custom, 304 SS): $350‑800
  • Hanging pleated screen: $200‑500
