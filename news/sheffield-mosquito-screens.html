Sheffield Mosquito Screens – Steel City, Parkland Homes & Student Housing

May 2, 2026 5 min read Sheffield, Parkland

Sheffield is known for its green spaces, hills, and two universities. From Victorian terraces in Crookes to modern flats in the city centre, each home type needs a slightly different insect screen solution. This guide covers the best options.

Victorian Terraces (Crookes, Walkley, Nether Edge)

These homes have tall sash or casement windows with original timber frames. Adhesive velcro screens are ideal – no drilling, removable, and invisible from outside. For owners wanting a more permanent solution, retractable screens that mount on the window lining are excellent.

Student & Rental Accommodation (Broomhill, Endcliffe, City Centre)

If you’re a student, you cannot drill. Magnetic mosquito nets for doors and adhesive screens for windows are perfect. They install in minutes and leave no damage – ideal for tenancy agreements.

Parkland Homes (Millhouses, Fulwood, Dore)

Homes near Sheffield’s many parks (Endcliffe Park, Graves Park) face higher mosquito and pollen levels. Fine mesh insect screens (22 holes per inch) block mosquitoes and also filter pollen – great for allergy sufferers. Consider top‑hung pleated screens for sliding doors that open onto gardens.

Allergy Benefits

Sheffield’s parks bring pollen. An insect screen with fine mesh (20‑22 holes per inch) can block up to 60% of airborne pollen, reducing hay fever symptoms while still allowing airflow.
