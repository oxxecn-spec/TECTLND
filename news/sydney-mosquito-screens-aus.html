Sydney Mosquito Screens – Retractable, Sliding & Pet‑Proof Solutions

May 2, 2026 6 min read Sydney, Retractable Screen

Sydney’s stunning harbour, beaches, and subtropical climate make outdoor living a joy – but mosquitoes, flies, and midges can quickly spoil the fun. Whether you live in a Victorian terrace in Paddington, a modern apartment in Barangaroo, or a coastal house in Cronulla, the right mosquito screen keeps bugs out while letting fresh air in. This guide covers the best solutions for Sydney homes.

Terrace Houses & Federation Homes (Inner West, Eastern Suburbs)

These heritage properties have tall sash or casement windows. Drilling into original timber is often discouraged. A custom mosquito screen with adhesive velcro mounting is ideal – no tools, no damage. For doors leading to small courtyards, a retractable screen door rolls up into a slim cassette and disappears when not in use. Pleated screen doors are another option; they fold like an accordion and slide to the side.

High‑Rise Apartments (CBD, Darling Harbour, Zetland)

New developments have large sliding windows and balcony doors. Sliding mosquito screens (also called sliding screen doors) are perfect – they glide on a low‑profile track and cover the entire opening. For a cleaner look, top‑hung pleated screens have no floor track. Many apartments also use magnetic mosquito screens on balcony doors – they snap shut automatically after you walk through.

Coastal & Beachside Homes (Northern Beaches, Sutherland Shire)

Salt air is tough on ordinary screens. You need 304 stainless steel mesh – it’s rust‑proof and lasts for decades. A heavy duty mosquito screen with reinforced frames is recommended for large openings. For homes with pets, a pet proof mosquito screen (304 mesh again) resists scratching from dogs and cats. Aluminum mosquito screens with powder‑coated frames are also popular for their durability.

Retractable & Roll‑Up Systems

A retractable mosquito screen rolls up into a cassette when not needed – perfect for living rooms where you want an unobstructed view. Roll up mosquito screens work the same way and are available for both windows and doors. For wide openings, folding mosquito screens (concertina style) slide sideways and fold into a compact stack.

Pet‑Friendly Options

If you have a dog or cat, standard fibreglass screens will tear within weeks. Choose a pet proof mosquito screen made from 304 stainless steel. It resists scratching, biting, and pushing. Many Sydney pet owners also install insect screen doors with heavy‑duty mesh on their back patios.

Custom Sizing for Non‑Standard Windows

Not all Sydney windows are standard sizes. A custom mosquito screen is the only way to get a perfect fit. MBORMC makes screens to your exact dimensions – whether you need a mosquito screen window, a door mosquito screen, or an insect screen window for a small bathroom vent. We also supply fly screen doors and mosquito mesh doors for rental properties.

Conclusion

Sydney homes benefit from retractable mosquito screens for living areas, sliding screen doors for balconies, and pet proof mosquito screens for active households.
