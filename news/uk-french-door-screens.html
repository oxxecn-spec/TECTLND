French Door Mosquito Screens – Bi‑Parting Solutions for UK Homes

May 2, 2026 5 min read French Door, Bi‑Parting

French doors – double doors that open inwards or outwards – are a beautiful feature in many UK homes, from Victorian terraces to modern extensions. But they are also a major entry point for insects. A single‑leaf screen doesn’t work. The answer is a bi‑parting folding screen (concertina).

Why a Single Screen Fails for French Doors

A standard retractable screen mounted on one side only covers one door leaf. When you open the other door, insects fly straight in. A hinged screen on the outside is impractical – it blocks handles and looks ugly. You need a system that covers both doors simultaneously, with no centre post.

How Bi‑Parting Folding Screens Work

Two folding panels (concertina style) are mounted on the inside of the door frame – one on the left, one on the right. Each screen can be pulled toward the centre, and they meet in the middle with a magnetic or overlap seal. You can open one side or both. When completely open, both screens fold back to the sides, leaving the doorway entirely clear – no centre post, no obstruction.

This design works for both inward‑opening and outward‑opening French doors.

Mesh Material Choices

  • PET polyester: Affordable, nearly invisible, good airflow. Suitable for low‑traffic French doors.
  • 304 stainless steel: Recommended for high‑traffic doors, pet owners, or homes where children push against screens. Also best for coastal areas.

Frames are powder‑coated aluminium – white, grey, black, or wood grain to match your doors.

Installation Overview

Each folding screen mounts with top and bottom tracks. A low‑profile bottom rail (approx 5mm high) is required. For tiled floors or heritage homes, ask about our top‑hung hanging system that has no floor track at all – the screen hangs from an overhead rail and glides without touching the floor. This is ideal for listed buildings or where you want to preserve original floor tiles.

Installation requires a drill and spirit level, but it’s manageable for a confident DIYer. MBORMC provides detailed instructions and video support.

Cost Range (Custom)

Bi‑parting folding screens are priced by total width and height. Expect £300‑800 depending on mesh material and size. Request a free quote with your door dimensions.

Why UK Homeowners Love Them

  • No central post – walk through with ease.
  • Suitable for conservation areas (internal installation, invisible from outside).
  • Works with both inward and outward opening doors.
  • Can be installed on existing French doors without replacing them.
