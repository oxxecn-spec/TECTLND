Mosquito Screens for UK Homes – A Complete Guide

May 2, 2026 5 min read UK Guide

Midges, mosquitoes, and flies are a nuisance across the UK – from the Scottish Highlands to Cornwall. A well‑fitted mosquito screen keeps them out while allowing fresh air in. This guide covers the best solutions for typical British windows and doors.

Sash Windows (Most Common in Older Homes)

Sash windows open vertically. The best screen is a retractable screen mounted at the top. Pull it down when the window is open; it rolls away when closed. Fixed half‑screens that cover only the lower sash are also popular. Avoid adhesive screens that might obstruct the sliding mechanism.

Casement & Tilt‑and‑Turn Windows

For outward‑opening windows, adhesive velcro screens are the easiest. They stick to the interior frame and don’t interfere with handles or hinges. Magnetic nets are another no‑drill option. For a permanent solution, a metal frame screen can be screwed onto the inside – measure carefully to allow the window to open fully.

French & Patio Doors

Bi‑parting folding screens (concertina) are ideal. Two panels slide from the centre to the sides, folding into a compact stack. They cover the entire opening without a central post. For sliding patio doors, a folding screen or a top‑hung pleated screen (no floor track) works best – especially on tiled floors.

Mesh Material Choices

  • PET polyester: Affordable, UV‑resistant, nearly invisible. Good for most UK homes.
  • 304 stainless steel: Pet‑proof and rust‑proof, ideal for coastal areas or if you have cats/dogs.

Installation Tips for UK Homes

Many British windows have deep recesses – perfect for adhesive screens. Always clean frames with alcohol before sticking. For removable screens, magnetic curtains are brilliant for kitchen doors. If you rent, adhesive and magnetic options leave no damage and can be taken down at the end of your tenancy.
